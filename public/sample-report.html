Executive Summary

Sunrise Family Dental demonstrates solid foundational HR practices but has significant gaps in documentation and I-9 compliance that create regulatory exposure. As a healthcare employer, you face heightened scrutiny from both DOL and state licensing boards. Your score of 324/500 places you in the MODERATE RISK category‚Äîbetter than average for small healthcare practices, but with three critical issues requiring immediate attention to avoid potential fines ranging from $252 to $25,000+ per violation.

Top 3 Priorities

1. Separate I-9 Forms from Personnel Files

ICE requires I-9s to be produced within 3 days of an audit notice‚Äîwithout exposing other employee records. Storing them in personnel files violates this requirement and can result in fines of $252-$2,507 per form, even if the forms themselves are correctly completed.

2. Formalize Performance Documentation

Your verbal-only approach to performance issues leaves you vulnerable to wrongful termination claims. In healthcare, where credentialing and patient safety are paramount, written documentation is essential for defending employment decisions.

3. Update Employee Handbook

A handbook last reviewed 3+ years ago likely lacks current COVID policies, updated harassment definitions, and recent state leave law changes. Outdated handbooks can actually create liability by establishing policies you no longer follow.

üî¥ Critical Issues

I-9 Storage Location CRITICAL
"In each employee's personnel file"

Risk: During an ICE audit, you must produce I-9s without exposing confidential employee information. Mixed storage makes this impossible and can result in fines of $252-$2,507 per form for first offenses, escalating to $2,507-$25,076 for repeat violations.

Fix: Create a dedicated I-9 binder or digital folder. Move all existing I-9s to this location. Implement a checklist ensuring new hire I-9s go directly to the separate file.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours to reorganize existing files

Performance Documentation CRITICAL
"Verbal conversations only"

Risk: Without written records, you cannot prove performance issues existed before termination. In wrongful termination suits, the burden is on the employer to demonstrate legitimate business reasons. Verbal warnings are nearly impossible to prove in court.

Fix: Implement a simple written warning form. Even a dated email summary sent after verbal discussions creates a paper trail. For healthcare staff, tie documentation to patient care standards where applicable.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 1 hour to create template, ongoing 10-15 min per incident

Employee Handbook Currency CRITICAL
"3+ years ago"

Risk: Employment law has changed significantly since 2022. Your handbook likely lacks required COVID policies, updated FMLA provisions, current harassment definitions, and recent state-specific leave laws. An outdated handbook can be used against you if it establishes expectations you're not meeting.

Fix: Have an employment attorney or HR consultant review and update your handbook. At minimum, add a statement that policies may be updated and the current version supersedes previous versions.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è Professional review: $500-1,500; Self-update with template: 4-6 hours

üü° Items Needing Attention

Harassment Training ATTENTION
"During onboarding only"

Risk: One-time training doesn't demonstrate ongoing good faith effort to prevent harassment. Many states now require annual training. In healthcare settings with power dynamics between providers and staff, regular training is especially important.

Fix: Implement annual harassment prevention training for all employees. Online training platforms offer affordable options ($15-30/employee). Document completion dates.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 1-2 hours annually per employee

Exit Interview Process ATTENTION
"Sometimes, inconsistently"

Risk: Inconsistent exit interviews mean you may miss warning signs of workplace issues and lack documentation of voluntary departures. In unemployment claims or wrongful termination suits, exit interview notes can be valuable evidence.

Fix: Create a standard exit interview form. Schedule exit interviews for all departing employees, even brief ones. Document voluntary resignation statements in writing.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 30 minutes per departing employee

Meal and Rest Break Policy ATTENTION
"Informal break expectations"

Risk: Many states require specific meal and rest breaks. In healthcare, patient care demands can lead to missed or interrupted breaks‚Äîwhich must still be compensated. Without clear policies, employees may have valid wage claims.

Fix: Research your state's break requirements. Create written policy specifying break times and procedures for patient care interruptions. Ensure time records capture actual breaks taken.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours to research and document policy

Retaliation Awareness ATTENTION
"Generally understood"

Risk: Retaliation is the #1 EEOC charge category. "Generally understood" isn't enough‚Äîmanagers need specific training on what constitutes retaliation, including subtle actions that may seem legitimate but appear retaliatory in context.

Fix: Include specific retaliation training in manager development. Document that managers understand they cannot take adverse action against employees who file complaints, even if discipline would otherwise be warranted.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 1-2 hours of focused manager training

Employment Poster Compliance ATTENTION
"Yes, but may be outdated"

Risk: Federal and state posting requirements change annually. Outdated posters can result in fines ranging from $100 to $35,000 depending on the violation. DOL inspectors often check poster compliance during any workplace visit.

Fix: Order current year federal and state poster sets. Many payroll providers include poster compliance services. Post in break room or other area accessible to all employees.

Effort: ‚è±Ô∏è 30 minutes to order and post; $30-75 for compliant poster set
